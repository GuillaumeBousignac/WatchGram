User Journey – Discovering and Comparing Watches on WatchGram

Person:
- Lucas, 22 years old, student with a passion for watches
- Objectives: Discover watches, easily compare them, and make an informed decision

---

Step 1 – Feed Exploration:
*Action:* Lucas opens WatchGram and browses the watch feed.
*User Objective:* To find inspiration and identify interesting models.
*Friction:* Too many models on the screen can be confusing.
*Opportunity:* Clear, visual feed focused solely on watches.

---

Step 2 – Social Interaction:
*Action:* He likes some watches and reads the comments.
*User Objective:* To confirm his interest through the reviews and interactions of other users.
*Friction:* On traditional social networks, technical information is often lacking.
*Opportunity:* To view both comments and technical data for each watch.

---

Step 3 – Saving Models:
*Action:* Lucas adds watches to his favorites for easy access.
*User Goal:* Organize his favorite models without wasting time.
*Friction:* On other platforms, favorites are neither centralized nor easily accessible.
*Opportunity:* Create a dedicated, easy-to-use, and well-structured favorites section.

--

Step 4 – Comparison:
*Action:** He selects 2 to 3 watches to compare side-by-side.
*User Goal:** Quickly see the differences in price, Diameter, Thickness, Water Resistance and Movement, etc.
*Friction:** Comparing on multiple sites requires time and attention.
*Opportunity:** Centralized and visual comparison in WatchGram.

--

Step 5 – Final Decision:
*Action:* Lucas identifies the watch that best suits him.
*User Goal:* Be sure of his choice before purchasing. *Friction:* Without a clear comparison, he may hesitate or give up.
*Opportunity:* A seamless experience that combines inspiration, information, and comparison, facilitating the decision.
