Benchmark – WatchGram

Existing solutions analyzed

1. Instagram
*Description :*
A general-purpose social platform widely used by watch brands and enthusiasts to share photos of watches.

*Strengths :*
- Very strong visual appeal
- Large existing community
- Social interaction (likes, comments)

*Limitations:*
- No structured data (price, diameter, movement, etc.)
- Impossible to compare watches technically
- Inefficient search for a purchase purpose

---

2. Chrono24
*Description:*
Marketplace specializing in buying and selling watches.

*Strengths:*
- Detailed technical data
- Actual market prices
- Large catalog

*Limitations:*
- Transaction-oriented interface, not very inspirational
- Limited social aspect
- Not very engaging for the initial browsing phase

---

3. Hodinkee
*Description:*
Media outlet specializing in watchmaking (articles, reviews, news).

*Strengths:*
- Expert and credible content
- In-depth model analysis

*Limitations:*
- Passive content consumption
- No personalization
- No direct comparison between models

---

WatchGram's Differentiation

WatchGram stands out due to:
- A "hybrid approach" combining social networking and decision-making tools
- The "centralization of comparable technical data"
- An "immersive visual experience" geared towards discovery
- A "simple comparison function," absent on traditional social networks

WatchGram thus occupies an intermediate space between inspiration, social engagement, and rationalized choice.
