User Interviews – WatchGram

Method
- 3 informal interviews with classmates
- Short discussions (10–15 minutes)
- Topic: Discovering, comparing, and choosing watches

---

Interview 1
*Profile:*
Student, interested in watches

*Needs:*
- Easily discover new models
- Quickly see important information

*Frustrations:*
- Information is spread across multiple websites
- Comparing two watches takes time

*Habits:*
- Uses Instagram to discover watches
- Then searches on Google

---

Interview 2
*Profile:*
Student, non-specialist

*Needs:*
- Understand the differences between watches
- Feel reassured before making a purchase

*Frustrations:*
- Too many technical terms
- Difficulty choosing between several models

*Habits:*
- Browses brand websites
- Compares prices online

---

Interview 3
*Profile:*
Student, design-oriented

*Needs:*
- Save watches he likes
- Easily find his favorites

*Frustrations:*
- Social media platforms are not organized for comparison
- Saved content is difficult to find

*Habits:*
- Uses Instagram for inspiration
- Saves images without any structure

---

Summary
- Users primarily use visual platforms for inspiration
- They need a simple tool for comparison Watches
- A solution combining "visual feed + comparison" addresses a real need
